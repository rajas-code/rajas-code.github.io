About me

Hi — I’m Rajapandi Nadar. I’m a physics researcher and teacher from Mumbai, fascinated by the universe’s most extreme phenomena. My work often revolves around pulsars, X-ray binaries, and the occasional gravitational-wave puzzle — cosmic clocks and fireworks that keep astrophysics exciting.

I’ve carried out research projects at TIFR and St. Xavier’s College, exploring pulsar timing, binary evolution, and high-energy telescope data. Alongside research, I actively conduct lectures in physics for both college and high-school students, sharing concepts in ways that make science approachable and engaging.

Beyond research and teaching, I enjoy building small projects (like estimating the Moon’s distance from photos), presenting my work at conferences, and finding creative ways to turn complex data into stories that reveal more about the cosmos.
